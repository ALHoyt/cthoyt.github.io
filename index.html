Science

I was trained in synthetic organic chemistry. Now, I'm using chemoinformatics for drug discovery for my PhD at the Bonn-Aachen International Center for Information Technology, University of Bonn.

Programming

For me, programming is about problem solving. I know a lot of languages and packages. Python and SciPy have been favorites lately. I love clean code, but I've been known to do anything for the one-liner.

Music

I'm in love with music. It carries me through a five-minute mile. I like dressing up fancy to see the symphony. I play sax and guitar, and dabble in everything else. I write music, too.

Writing

My freshman year of college summer reading was Atul Gawande's Better. He insisted at the importance of writing. I was introduced to Derek Lowe's blog, In the Pipeline, on the first day of my GSK internship in 2012. I haven't missed a post since.

As I transitioned into informatics, I wished there was a publication like In the Pipeline. Sadly there isn't, so I will try my best to write about my work in graduate school. For now, I'm writing about my adventures in Europe as a teaching assistant on Northeastern's Chemistry, Drug Discovery, and Development Dialogue.
